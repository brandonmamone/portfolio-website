Quick Nest

My Process

Ideation

I designed the app with principals like trust, encouragement, and ease in mind. I find that financial responsibility can be daunting and wanted something easy to use and intuitive. I set out to design graphics that visually depicted ones savings and expenses while still maintaining a secure feel so that users felt safe inputting financial data.

User Flow

The user flow helped visulaize the overall layout of the app. I wanted a home screen that displayed most of the information one would need to look at their savings and more detailed options on other screens. One of the major challenges would be to design this without making things too cluttered and hard to comprehend at a glance.

Wireframes

The early design stages featured a splash screen leading to a login screen. The user would have the option to create a profile with an email/password or user their Google/Apple account. I knew I wanted the home page to display most of the information a user would want to see a t a glance and navigation to more detailed screens.

The mid stages of the design process had me blocking out the shapes and determining the grid. It was important to feature the graphs as large and half the screen as I wanted the user to be able to quickly see their savings, expenses, and goal progress without having to scroll.

Mockups

Quick Nest features a wallet which displays their payments broken down into categories, their target financial goals with progress bars, and a customizable profile editing screen.
